ARR Waterfall & Retention Engine
ARR Movement Waterfall — FY2021A to FY2025A
Starting ARR → Logo Churn → Gross Retained → Expansion → Contraction → New Logos → Ending ARR · $millions
The seat model in action: FY2024 was the NRR trough — contraction spiked to $138M as customers downsized during the pricing transition, while logo churn also hit 8.9%. FY2025 shows both reversing simultaneously: contraction fell to $158M on a larger base (improving rate), expansion grew to $435M, and new logo ARR held at $419M. That dual reversal is why NRR recovered from ~102% to ~104.5%. The structural logic: every new hire at a HubSpot customer automatically adds seat ARR — no sales motion required.
NRR Recovery Trajectory
Dollar-Based Net Revenue Retention · FY2021A–FY2025A
Peak → trough → recovery: NRR peaked at ~115% (FY2021 IPO era), compressed to ~102% during SMB macro headwinds and seat model transition, and is now recovering. FY2025 at ~104.5% marks the first YoY improvement since FY2022. The seat model creates structural expansion: upmarket deals (>$10K MRR) grew +41% YoY in FY2025 and now represent ~45% of revenue.

New Logo Acquisition Funnel
PLG vs Field Sales contribution · FY2025A
PLG structural advantage: ~70% of new logos acquired via freemium at near-zero marginal S&M. Field sales generates 30% of logos but at ~$65K CAC vs ~$0 PLG CAC. The blended Magic Number of 0.36x is artificially low — PLG-adjusted (field S&M only) is 1.19x, which is healthy for this hybrid model.

Magic Number — Blended vs PLG-Adjusted
Net New ARR / S&M spend · FY2022A–FY2025A
The PLG adjustment matters. Blended Magic Number (0.36–0.68x) includes all S&M — brand, digital, partner — even though ~70% of new logos require near-zero incremental spend. Adjusting to field-only S&M (~30% of total) reveals 1.19–2.28x, squarely in "healthy" territory. Present both when asked; the blended number without context is misleading.
Rule of 40
Revenue Growth % + FCF Margin % · Annual
Approaching the 40% threshold. FY2025 scores 38.2% (19.2% growth + 19.0% FCF margin). FY2022 was a false positive at 35% driven by high growth masking negative FCF. The current trajectory — growth moderating, FCF expanding — is the more durable path to crossing 40%. Base case FY2026E: ~35%; Bull case: 41.5%.

Key Risks
Watch factors for bear scenario
AI monetization: Breeze agents early-stage; uncertain attach rate and pricing power in FY2026. 8K activations FY2025 vs target 50K+.
Upmarket execution: Moving from SMB → mid-market lengthens sales cycles. Risk of pipeline miss in ramp quarters.
SMB macro sensitivity: If US enters recession, SMB logo churn ticks from 12.5% → 15%; ~$150M ARR at risk.
FX headwind: 40%+ international revenue; stronger USD = 100–200bps reported vs constant-currency spread.
Key Catalysts
Watch factors for bull scenario
Breeze AI monetization: 8K activations FY2025 → 50K+ in FY2026; outcome-based pricing could add $200–400M incremental ARR with near-zero marginal S&M.
Upmarket momentum: Deals >$10K MRR +41% in FY2025; +$5K MRR deals +33%. If this sustains, ASRPC inflects to +6% vs +3.3% in FY2025.
Rule of 40 crossover: Bull case crosses 40% — triggers institutional re-rating toward Salesforce/ServiceNow premium (13–15x NTM).
$1B buyback: 24-month program authorized Feb 2026; signals confidence + reduces float. ~$300M est. deployed in FY2026.
