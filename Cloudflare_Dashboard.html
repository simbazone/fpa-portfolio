Implied Share Price by Valuation Method

Football field shows Bear/Base/Bull implied price per method. Reference: $197.15 (Dec 31 2025 closing). DCF uses EV/Revenue exit multiples (sell-side standard for high-growth SaaS). EV/FCF comps less relevant pre-GAAP profitability.

⚠ Why is the M&A range lower than DCF and trading comps? Three methods measure three different things. DCF ($72–$275) captures intrinsic value based on future cash flows — what the business is fundamentally worth. Trading comps ($44–$125) reflect what the market currently pays for comparable public companies. M&A analysis ($43–$66) reflects what acquirers have historically paid in actual deals — and precedent transactions use LTM revenue multiples (7–11x) that are structurally lower than where high-growth SaaS companies trade publicly today. The Cloudflare-specific kicker: Matthew Prince and Michelle Zatlyn together control 50.6% of voting power through Class B shares (10 votes per share vs. 1 vote for public Class A). That makes hostile takeover mathematically impossible — a deal only happens if the founders want to sell. This removes the normal control premium dynamic, shrinks the realistic acquirer universe, and structurally compresses the implied takeout premium relative to intrinsic value. In short: the M&A range is low for two compounding reasons — precedent deal multiples are structurally conservative, and Cloudflare is a structurally non-acquirable asset. That's not a modeling error; it's the correct read of the capital structure.

Executive Leadership & Commentary

Thomas Seifert, CFO — Key Financial Themes
📉
On Gross Margin Compression
“Operating margin is becoming the better measure of competitiveness versus gross margin. The shift from free to paid traffic is P&L neutral — costs move from S&M into cost of revenue, temporarily compressing gross margin while preserving profitability. Workers products carry lower gross margins today but equivalent unit economics when viewed below gross profit.”
Q1 2026 Earnings Call · CFO Prepared Remarks
⚙️
On Restructuring & FCF
“The workforce reduction of ~20% is not a cost-cutting exercise. It is a re-architecture for the agentic AI era. Quota-carrying sales force capacity is explicitly protected and growing. Free cash flow guidance for full year 2026 remains UNCHANGED despite $140–150M in restructuring charges. Efficiency and productivity will scale faster than revenue as a result.”
Q1 2026 Earnings Call · CFO Prepared Remarks
🎯
On Rule of 40 & Long-Term Model
“With the guidance in place today, we are getting north of 46% from a Rule of 40 perspective. We have visibility to reaching north of 50% next year. Our Updated Long-Term Operating Model targets non-GAAP operating margin of 30%+ and FCF margin of 30–35%+ — meaningfully above our prior targets of 20%+ and ~25% respectively.”
